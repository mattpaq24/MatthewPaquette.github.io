INTERACTION DESIGN

Plan Creation Tool

Context

THE COMPANY

TREES helps new undergrad students ease in to university life. Students take an introductory questionnaire and are recommended step-by-step action plans based on their responses.

THE PROJECT

Prior to this project, plans could only be created by university administrators through a desktop-exclusive tool.

To bring more action plans to the TREES mobile app, I was asked to design a tool that would allow for users to create action plans on mobile devices.

Logistics

Goal: Design a plan creation tool (PCT) that allows users to create-step-by-step action plans from their mobile devices

Process: In building the PCT, I conducted:

  • Research, Interviews
  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing
  • Handoff to developers

Team: This was a solo UX Project

Timeline: Research to handoff took roughly two months

Research

INTERVIEWING

Beyond considering the needs of plan creators, I felt that understanding the needs of plan consumers (university students) should inform the structure of the PCT. This way, I could design the PCT so that plan creators can more easily create plans that align with consumer needs.

To understand both consumer and creator needs, I talked to students and advisors from three private universities in Nova Scotia, and two public universities in California.

I conducted one-on-one interviews, and led a focus group consisting of peer leaders at one of the schools in Nova Scotia.

SIGNIFIGANT TAKEAWAYS AND INSIGHTS

  • Students feel a greater sense of belonging on campus when they are connected to real people. The PCT should incentivize the creation of plans that lead to interaction, not a substitute.
  • Students have difficulty engaging with school resources when they have to traverse through a myriad of portals and web pages. I should consider the organizational systems students use, and build the PCT so that creators make plans that allow consumers to integrate the information withing plans into their existing organizational systems.
